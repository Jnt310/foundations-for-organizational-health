## Purpose

The Research Agenda captures the major questions, hypotheses, constructs, and evidence-building priorities for Foundations for Organizational Health™.

## Primary Research Question

What are the leading organizational indicators that predict whether a mission-driven helping organization will remain clinically or service-effective, financially viable, ethically grounded, and workforce-sustainable over the next three to five years?

## Initial Research Hypotheses

### Hypothesis 1 — Organizational Well-Being as a Leading Indicator
Organizations with low organizational well-being scores will demonstrate elevated risk for turnover, burnout, leadership fatigue, implementation failure, quality decline, and ethical erosion.

### Hypothesis 2 — Culture as Infrastructure
Culture is not separate from operational performance. Psychological safety, trust, communication, and learning capacity influence implementation success, workforce sustainability, quality, and ethical practice.

### Hypothesis 3 — Compliance Is Necessary but Insufficient
Compliance metrics alone do not adequately predict organizational health, sustainability, staff well-being, or ethical practice.

### Hypothesis 4 — Leadership Creates Conditions
Leadership behaviors influence culture, staff voice, learning capacity, accountability, and the organization’s ability to sustain change.

### Hypothesis 5 — Learning Capacity Predicts Adaptability
Organizations that identify, discuss, and learn from mistakes are more likely to adapt successfully and less likely to repeat avoidable failures.

## Priority Constructs for Development

| Construct | Working Definition | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Organizational Health | The degree to which an organization can fulfill its mission sustainably, ethically, effectively, and without chronic harm to its people. | Developing |
| Organizational Well-Being | The condition of the organization’s internal environment as experienced through trust, psychological safety, voice, workload sustainability, leadership credibility, and mission alignment. | Incubator |
| Organizational Capacity to Care | The organization’s ability to care for employees, patients/clients, communities, and its own long-term mission. | Developing |
| Learning Capacity | The organization’s ability to detect, discuss, learn from, and respond to mistakes, feedback, and changing conditions. | Developing |
| Ecosystem Stress Signals | Early signals that the organizational ecosystem is under strain before lagging indicators decline. | Incubator |
| Ethical Climate | The degree to which organizational conditions support ethical decision-making, transparency, and protection from harmful practices. | Planned |

## Research Questions

1. What organizational conditions consistently precede burnout and turnover in behavioral healthcare?
2. What organizational conditions precede ethical drift or unethical practices?
3. Can culture vitality predict implementation success?
4. Can organizational well-being predict leadership fatigue?
5. Which indicators are leading indicators versus lagging indicators?
6. How can staff voice and psychological safety be measured practically?
7. What is the relationship between financial pressure and ethical risk?
8. How do organizations distinguish accountability from blame?
9. What does a healthy organizational ecosystem look like across different helping professions?
10. What universal conditions are required for sustainable care?
